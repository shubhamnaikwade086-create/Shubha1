UPSC Personality Test (Stage III)

The Personality Test, commonly known as the Interview, is the final and crucial stage of the Civil Services Examination. It is not an examination of specific knowledge, which has already been tested in the Mains, but an assessment of the candidate's personality and suitability for a career in public service.

Assessment Details

  • **Total Marks:** 275 marks.
  • **Assessment Body:** A Board of competent and impartial observers who have wide experience in public affairs.
  • **Objective:** To judge the mental calibre of a candidate, which includes intellectual qualities, social traits, and interest in current affairs.

Key Focus Areas

The interview questions are often based on the candidate's Detailed Application Form (DAF) and current affairs. The board assesses qualities like:

  • **Mental Alertness:** Quick thinking and reasoning under pressure.
  • **Critical Powers of Assimilation:** Ability to process information and form a balanced judgement.
  • **Clarity and Logical Exposition:** Clear communication and presentation of ideas.
  • **Integrity and Moral Courage:** Honesty in expression and a strong ethical foundation.
  • **Balance of Judgement:** Ability to see multiple sides of a complex issue.
  • **Social Cohesion:** Leadership and contribution to public welfare.

The marks secured in the Personality Test are added to the Mains marks to determine the final rank and service allocation.
