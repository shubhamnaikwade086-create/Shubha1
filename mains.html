UPSC Mains Examination (Written Stage)

The Mains examination is the most crucial stage as it determines a candidate's position on the final merit list. It consists of nine papers, all of which are descriptive in nature and test a candidate's knowledge, articulation, and analytical skills.

Merit Ranking Papers (Total: 1750 Marks)

These seven papers are counted for the final rank calculation:

  • Paper I: Essay (250 Marks) - Requires writing two essays on various topics, testing clarity of thought and coherence.
  • Paper II: General Studies I (GS 1 - 250 Marks) - Covers Indian Heritage and Culture, History, and Geography of the World and Society.
  • Paper III: General Studies II (GS 2 - 250 Marks) - Covers Governance, Constitution, Polity, Social Justice, and International Relations.
  • Paper IV: General Studies III (GS 3 - 250 Marks) - Covers Technology, Economic Development, Biodiversity, Environment, Security, and Disaster Management.
  • Paper V: General Studies IV (GS 4 - 250 Marks) - Covers Ethics, Integrity, and Aptitude. Includes theoretical questions and case studies.
  • Paper VI & VII: Optional Subject (Paper 1 & 2 - 250 Marks each) - Two papers on the single optional subject chosen by the candidate (total 500 marks).

Qualifying Papers (Mandatory)

These two papers must be cleared with a minimum qualifying mark (25% each), but their marks are not counted towards the final merit total:

  • Paper A: Indian Language (300 Marks) - Any language listed in the Eighth Schedule of the Constitution.
  • Paper B: English (300 Marks) - Standard English comprehension and grammar.

Candidates must ensure they achieve the minimum required marks in these qualifying papers, otherwise their GS and Optional papers will not be evaluated.
